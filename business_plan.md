## 1. Executive Summary

**MadnessBot** is an AI-powered assistant and inventory management tool designed specifically for automotive mechanics. Unlike generic AI tools, MadnessBot is built with deep industry context, offering features like "recursive" real-time web searching for parts and technical service bulletins (TSBs), image analysis for diagnostics, and a revolutionary toolbox inventory management system.

Currently developed and operated by a single founder with deep ties to the industry (working on a Snap-on tool truck), MadnessBot has a working product with a clear value proposition: empowering mechanics to work faster, smarter, and more profitably.

We are seeking **$100,000 in seed funding** to provide 12 months of runway. This capital is critical to allow the founder to **exit their current role at Snap-on** (which views MadnessBot as a direct competitor to their Mitchell1 subsidiary) and operate the company full-time. This lean approach ensures we raise only what is absolutely necessary to reach profitability and sustainability.

**Mission:** To empower every technician with the knowledge and tools of a master mechanic.

## 3. Products and Services

MadnessBot solves the critical information gap mechanics face daily. Traditional information systems (AllData, Mitchell1) are expensive and static. Generic AI (ChatGPT) lacks specific automotive context and real-time parts data.

### 3.1 Key Features
- **AI Master Mechanic:** A 24/7 assistant that diagnoses problems, explains procedures, and offers "master tech" advice.
- **Toolbox Inventory Management:** A game-changing feature that tracks what tools a mechanic owns. It can cross-reference a job (e.g., "Timing belt on 2018 Accord") with the user's inventory to identify missing tools *before* the job starts.
- **Real-Time Web Search:** "Recursively" searches multiple suppliers (AutoZone, NAPA, RockAuto, etc.) for live parts pricing, availability, and TSBs.
- **Image Analysis:** Mechanics can upload photos of worn parts, wiring diagrams, or work orders for instant analysis and diagnostic plans.
- **Video Demonstrations:** AI-generated short videos (4-25 seconds) showing specific repair procedures on demand.
- **Bilingual Support:** Full support for English and Spanish, expanding the market significantly.

### 3.2 Pricing Model
- **Subscription:** $49.95 per month.
- **Trial:** 7-day free trial (full access).
- **Differentiation:** Significantly more affordable than professional shop manuals (often $150+/mo) while offering interactive, intelligent assistance.

## 4. Market Opportunity

### 4.1 Market Size & Opportunity
The automotive repair industry in the United States represents a **$77-202 billion market** (2024 estimates vary by scope)[1][2], with approximately **176,000-299,000 automotive repair and maintenance shops**[3][4] employing approximately **650,000-1,000,000 technicians**[5][6].

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):** All automotive technicians in the US.
- **650,000-1,000,000 professional technicians**[5][6] × $49.95/month = **$390-600 million annual recurring revenue (ARR)** potential.
- This includes dealership techs, independent shop mechanics, and mobile technicians.

**Serviceable Available Market (SAM):** Independent and small shop mechanics who need affordable solutions.
- **~325,000-500,000 technicians** (50% of total, excluding large dealership techs with free access to OEM systems).
- Potential: **$195-300 million ARR**.

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM):** Early adopters and our immediate reach.
- **Year 1 Target: 500 users** = $300k ARR (0.08% market penetration).
- **Year 3 Target: 5,000 users** = $3M ARR (0.8% market penetration).
- **Year 5 Target: 25,000 users** = $15M ARR (4% market penetration).

### 4.2 Target Customer Profile
**Primary Persona: "Mike the Independent Tech"**
- **Age:** 28-45
- **Experience:** 5-20 years in the field
- **Income:** $45k-$75k/year (paid flat-rate/book time)
- **Pain Points:**
  - Wastes 30-60 min/day looking up parts, TSBs, or hunting for tools.
  - Can't afford Mitchell1 ($150+/mo) but needs more than YouTube.
  - Loses money when he doesn't have the right tool and has to stop mid-job.
- **Buying Behavior:** Will pay for tools that make them money. Skeptical of software unless it's proven.
- **Social Media:** Active on Instagram/TikTok for automotive content.

**Secondary Personas:**
- **Fleet Mechanics:** Work on commercial vehicles, need fast answers.
- **DIY Enthusiasts:** Hobbyists who want pro-level advice.
- **Spanish-speaking Techs:** Underserved by existing tools (MadnessBot's bilingual support is a huge advantage).

### 4.3 Market Needs & Pain Points
1. **Speed = Money:** Mechanics are paid by "book time." If a job books 2 hours but takes 3 because they couldn't find a part number or were missing a tool, they lose money.
2. **Tool Chaos:** The average mechanic owns $25k-$50k in tools but doesn't have a system to track them. "Where's my 10mm?" is a daily crisis.
3. **Information Gap:** Existing solutions are either too expensive (Mitchell1), too generic (ChatGPT), or too slow (forums/YouTube).
4. **Bilingual Gap:** Spanish-speaking mechanics are massively underserved by existing tools.

### 4.4 Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Price | MadnessBot Advantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

**MadnessBot's Unique Moats:**
1. **Inventory Intelligence:** No competitor tracks a mechanic's toolbox and cross-references it with job requirements.
2. **Recursive Search:** Our proprietary "recursive" search algorithm queries multiple suppliers simultaneously for live pricing.
3. **Industry Insider:** The founder's credibility and network provide a distribution advantage incumbents can't replicate.

### 4.5 Market Trends Favoring MadnessBot
- **AI Adoption in Trades:** Blue-collar workers are increasingly comfortable with AI tools (ChatGPT usage among trades is up 300% YoY).
- **Flat-Rate Squeeze:** Shops are cutting labor times, forcing techs to work faster. Tools that save time are essential.
- **Tool Truck Debt:** Mechanics are drowning in tool debt. A $50/mo subscription that prevents buying duplicate tools pays for itself immediately.
- **Generational Shift:** Younger techs (under 35) expect software solutions and are willing to pay for productivity.

### 7.2 Unit Economics & Cost Analysis
To ensure profitability, we have modeled the "Cost Per Query" (CPQ) based on current AI API pricing (e.g., OpenAI GPT-4o, Anthropic Claude 3.5 Sonnet) and search API costs.

**Cost Drivers per Interaction:**
- **Input Tokens:** Context window (chat history, system prompt, RAG data). Avg: 2,000 tokens.
- **Output Tokens:** The AI's response. Avg: 500 tokens.
- **Search API:** Cost to query live web data (Bing/Google).
- **Image/Video:** Higher cost for vision analysis or video generation.

**Estimated Costs:**
| Interaction Type | Frequency | Cost Estimate | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Text Query** | 70% | $0.015 | Basic diagnostics, Q&A. |
| **RAG/Search Query** | 20% | $0.025 | Requires web search + context processing. |
| **Image/Video Analysis** | 10% | $0.040 | Vision API or video generation costs. |
| **Blended CPQ** | **100%** | **$0.020** | Weighted average cost per query. |

**Monthly Margin Analysis:**
| User Type | Queries/Month | Monthly Cost | Subscription | Gross Margin |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Average User** | 100 (5/day) | $2.00 | $49.95 | **96% ($47.95)** |
| **Power User** | 400 (20/day) | $8.00 | $49.95 | **84% ($41.95)** |
| **Extreme User** | 1,000 (50/day) | $20.00 | $49.95 | **60% ($29.95)** |

*Even with heavy usage, the unit economics remain highly positive. The $49.95 price point provides a substantial buffer against API price fluctuations.*
